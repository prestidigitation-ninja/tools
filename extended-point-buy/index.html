Extended Point Buy for D&D 5E

Points Remaining

/

The default Extended Point Buy costs have been generated using a cubic regression formula that follows the standard costs, while extending the curve for both lower and higher scores. This is to enforce the rapidly increasing cost for scores above 15 and rapidly decreasing cost for scores below 8.

By default, we do not allow negative score costs. This is done to avoid potentially imbalanced characters that could result from deducting points from lower scores to augment higher ones. This is an opinionated choice by the creator aiming to promote balanced and fairly distributed abilities across characters.

However, this system is flexible and designed with customization in mind. As the Dungeon Master, if you wish to adjust the rule to better suit your campaign style, feel free to override this default setting. By enabling the ‘Allow Negative Score Costs’ option, you can permit ability scores to have a negative cost.

Please note, while there are no official rules for such an extension, this formula offers a practical approach to extend flexibility in character creation. Feel free to customise the score costs as per your campaign requirements.
